## TikTok launches UK Creator Card with Visa

Social media platform TikTok has launched a new debit card for content creators in the UK to manage earnings from TikTok LIVE and brand deals. The new Creator Card has been developed in partnership with Visa for cardholders to better track cash flow and separate professional expenses.

Lucy Demery, SVP and head of Visa commercial solutions in Europe, comments that the UK launch of the Creator Card is "designed to give creators faster access to income from TikTok LIVE, brand partnerships, and platform payouts", allowing cardholders to "spend, plan, and reinvest in their business straight away".

TikTok continues to expand its financial footprint globally, having also applied for Electronic Money Institution and direct credit licences in Brazil earlier this month.

## Mexico's Plata hits $5bn valuation following $405m Series C

Mexican challenger bank Plata has achieved a valuation of $5 billion following a $405 million Series C funding round, establishing itself as Latin America's most valuable privately held digital bank.

Plata was previously valued at $1.5 billion after a $160 million Series A in March 2025, before expanding to a $3.1 billion valuation following a $250 million Series B just six months ago.

The latest funding round, which brings Plata's total debt and equity raised to $2 billion, was led by Bicycle Capital with participation from existing backers Audeo Ventures, Kora, Hedosophia, and Spice Expeditions, among others.

## Monzo's chief banking officer Kunal Malani steps down to head Chase UK

Kunal Malani, Monzo's group general manager and chief banking officer since 2020, is stepping down to head up JP Morgan's operations in the UK.

Announcing his departure on LinkedIn, Malani writes: "After an incredible 6-year journey at Monzo, I've decided it’s time for a new adventure. I’m thrilled to share that I will be stepping into the role of Head of Chase UK, CEO JPMEL [JP Morgan Europe Limited] in the coming months."

In his new position at Chase UK, Malani will succeed Kuba Fast, who has led the UK operation since May 2024. Chase launched its UK division in 2021 and recently surpassed three million customers.

## Kraken parent Payward to buy Bitnomial in deal worth up to $550m

Payward, the parent company of crypto exchange Kraken, says it has inked a deal to acquire digital asset derivatives exchange Bitnomial "for up to $550 million payable in cash and stock".

The acquisition is expected to close "in the first half of 2026", Payward says, and includes Bitnomial’s three US Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) licences: Designated Contract Market (DCM), Derivatives Clearing Organisation (DCO), and Futures Commission Merchant (FCM).

"With the acquisition of Bitnomial, Payward now offers a regulated derivatives offering across all of its major markets," the company continues.

## Temenos hires Daniel Schmucki as CFO

Temenos has appointed Daniel Schmucki as the company's new chief financial officer (CFO), effective 3 August 2026. The leadership transition was announced in line with Temenos' Q1 2026 results, which show a 13% rise in annual recurring revenue to $861 million.

Schmucki will take over from Takis Spiliopoulos, who has served as CFO at Temenos since April 2019. Spiliopoulos was elevated to interim CEO in September following the departure of Jean-Pierre Brulard, before taking on the position permanently in December.

Temenos says Schmucki "will lead on advancing the finance function to the next stage to continuously drive efficiency and productivity, including the roll out of AI initiatives".
