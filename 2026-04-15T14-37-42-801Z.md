## Revolut fined €11m by Italian competition authority over "unfair commercial practices"

The Italian competition authority, Autorità Garante della Concorrenza e del Mercato (AGCM), has issued an €11 million fine on Revolut over alleged "unfair commercial practices" and violations of Italian consumer protection laws, according to an AGCM statement.

The AGCM asserts that Revolut did not provide Italian clients with "sufficient pre-contractual information, or advance notice before restrictions were imposed, nor did they provide an opportunity to respond or adequate assistance once restrictions had been imposed".

In comments sent to FinTech Futures, a Revolut spokesperson states: "Revolut strongly disagrees with the AGCM's findings and will appeal the decision in the Italian courts."

## FundApps CEO Andrew White steps down, Frederic Ceyte appointed successor

Andrew White has stepped down as CEO of UK-based regtech FundApps after 16 years of leadership. The company reins now pass to fintech veteran Frederic Ceyte, effective immediately.

In a LinkedIn statement announcing the leadership transition, FundApps says Ceyte will work to "drive platform unification, expand market presence, and accelerate international growth".

As part of the leadership change, White is transitioning to a non-executive director role within the company. Under White’s leadership, FundApps has grown to monitor over $30 trillion in assets under management across more than 100 jurisdictions.

## Coinbase national trust bank charter conditionally approved by OCC

The US Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) has granted conditional approval for a National Trust Company Charter to cryptocurrency exchange Coinbase.

In a statement, Greg Tusar, co-CEO of Coinbase Institutional, says the conditional approval will "bring consistency and uniformity to our custody business", which is to operate under Coinbase National Trust Company.

With conditional approval now in hand, Tusar says the additional federal oversight will "create a foundation for new products", with the offering to cover "payments and related services" for institutions and individuals.

## Fintech super-app Bolt cuts workforce by approximately 30%

BlackRock-backed financial super-app Bolt continues to boil down internal talent pools with new plans to cut its current workforce by approximately 30%.

A company spokesperson has confirmed the move to FinTech Futures, stating that staff were informed of the cuts late last week. The spokesperson declined to disclose when the chop would take place or why the move was being actioned.

It is also unknown how many personnel will be affected by the move. Bolt has induced a series of major layoffs in recent years, including most recently in December 2023, when reports suggested that 29%, or then over 100 people, had been let go.

Bolt has remained well capitalised throughout its lifetime, and has raised nearly $1 billion among a cohort of institutional investors featuring BlackRock, Schonfeld, Invus Opportunities, H.I.G. Growth, CE Innovation Capital, Activant Capital, and Moore Strategic Ventures, among others.

## Indian fintech KreditBee hits $1.5bn valuation with $280m Series E

KreditBee, an online credit solutions provider based in India, has raised $280 million in a Series E funding round co-led by Hornbill Capital and Motilal Oswal, as the company prepares for an initial public offering (IPO).

The round, which also drew participation from Advent International, Premji Invest, MUFG, and other institutional backers, has cemented KreditBee's unicorn status, valuing the company at $1.5 billion.

According to comments made by co-founder and CEO Madhusudan Ekambaram to The Times of India, the cash injection will primarily be used to strengthen the firm's lending book and balance sheet in preparation for its IPO, rather than for product development.
