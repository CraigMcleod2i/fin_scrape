## OpenAI snaps up AI personal finance start-up Hiro

OpenAI has expanded its acquisition portfolio with the purchase of US-based AI personal finance fintech Hiro. The financial details of the transaction have not been disclosed.

Founded by co-CEOs Ethan Bloch and Rushabh Doshi in 2024, Hiro offers a solution that integrates data from users' financial products, such as credit cards and savings accounts, and then acts as a personal assistant to provide tailored financial recommendations and answer queries.

"We started Hiro with the vision of building an AI personal CFO, and we worked relentlessly to make it real," Bloch said in a statement on LinkedIn. "As Rushabh Doshi and I got to know the team at OpenAI, it became clear that joining forces would give us the opportunity to pursue that vision at a much larger scale."

## HKMA grants first Hong Kong stablecoin licences to Standard Chartered JV and HSBC

The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) has granted Hong Kong's first stablecoin issuer licences to HSBC and Anchorpoint Financial Limited, a joint venture involving Standard Chartered, HKT Limited, and Animoca Brands Limited.

The regulator says Anchorpoint and HSBC "demonstrated their capabilities to properly manage risks with credible use cases and development plans, in addition to meeting the relevant licensing requirements under the ordinance".

Both newly licenced issuers initially plan to issue Hong Kong Dollar (HKD)-pegged stablecoins, with applications spanning cross-border payments, local and conditional payments, tokenised asset trading, and supply chain financing.

## Exclusive: CredAble appoints Ashutosh Taparia as CEO of its fintech business

Ashutosh Taparia has been appointed CEO of CredAble’s fintech business, as the company scales its AI-led working capital infrastructure and accelerates expansion across Europe, the Americas, APAC, and MENA, FinTech Futures exclusively revealed this week.

The former HSBC executive takes charge of a division that builds and deploys cloud-native working capital platforms that allow banks and large corporates to onboard, underwrite, and execute digital disbursements of working capital financing.

CredAble's fintech business, comprising more than 300 team members, was formerly led by company co‑founder Nirav Choksi, who is now moving to the role of chairman.

## Bolt Group acquires Aussie consumer payments app Beem

Australian fintech Bolt Group has acquired Beem, a consumer payments app, from payments infrastructure operator Australian Payments Plus (AP+) for an undisclosed fee. Following the deal, AP+ says it will work closely with Bolt "to transition services by the end of June 2026”.

Beem is a peer-to-peer (P2P) payments app that lets users pay, request, and transfer money without the need for account numbers or bank state branch (BSB) codes, processing more than $6 billion in transactions since launching in 2018.

James Woodward, CEO of Bolt Group Australia, says the fintech aims to "evolve Beem into a true everyday money app — bringing together payments, a gateway to transaction and savings accounts, multi-currency, and investing”.

## UK fintech Hokodo closes down, founders launch new venture Liquidity Lab

UK-based B2B buy now, pay later (BNPL) fintech Hokodo has closed down, according to an announcement by the company on LinkedIn.

Established in 2018, Hokodo provided B2B merchants with real-time trade credit solutions, offering flexible trade accounts and instalment plans with terms ranging from 30, 60, and 90 days.

Following the closure of Hokodo, the company's founders are set to continue working together on a new venture called Liquidity Lab.

In its LinkedIn post, Hokodo says: "The founders are carrying eight years of lessons into a new venture: Liquidity Lab, a specialist consulting firm helping B2B companies transform their trade credit and cash flow operations using AI."
