## Vestwell doubles valuation with $385m Series E

Workplace savings fintech Vestwell has raised $385 million in a Series E funding round led by Blue Owl Capital and Sixth Street Growth.

According to a company statement, the raise "doubles Vestwell's valuation since its 2023 Series D and brings total capital raised to $660 million".

Vestwell secured $125 million in its Series D round in 2023, with Bloomberg reporting at the time that the funding was raised at a company valuation of around $1 billion.

The company says funds from the Series E have been earmarked for three specific purposes: expanding its platform, strengthening integrations with payroll and HR systems, and scaling distribution through financial institutions and partners.

## PSR fines Bank of Ireland UK £3.7m over CoP implementation delay

The Payment Systems Regulator (PSR) has imposed a fine of £3,779,300 on Bank of Ireland UK (BOIUK) for failing to implement Confirmation of Payee (CoP) by the required deadline in 2023.

The regulator says BOIUK, a wholly-owned UK subsidiary of Bank of Ireland, was "directed to have a system in place to send and receive CoP requests by 31 October 2023", but missed this deadline by 14 months.

As a result of the delay, the PSR says "the safeguard was not applied to transactions involving more than 1.14 million new payees, with payments totalling approximately £6.9 billion".

In comments sent to FinTech Futures, a BOIUK spokesperson states: "Bank of Ireland UK fully acknowledges and sincerely apologises for the delay in implementing send requests for Confirmation of Payee, which has been in place for all customers since January 2025. The Bank takes its regulatory obligations extremely seriously and regrets that this issue arose."

## Former Uber exec Tristan Kirchner named CEO of ClearBank's European operations

ClearBank has appointed payments veteran Tristan Kirchner as CEO of its European arm, ClearBank Europe N.V., effective immediately.

Kirchner brings experience from Barclays, Visa, American Express, and Uber, where he has worked since 2022, notably serving as the company's global director of payments for almost two years.

Based in Amsterdam, Kirchner will oversee ClearBank's European operations and long‑term strategy, including its expansion efforts across the continent.

Since landing its Credit Institution Licence from the European Central Bank in 2024, ClearBank Europe has grown to support 35 clients, passported its services into 20 European countries, and is now launching a French branch.

## Stripe's Bridge secures conditional OCC approval for national trust bank charter

Stripe's stablecoin infrastructure firm Bridge has received conditional approval from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) to establish a federally chartered national trust bank.

Under the national trust bank charter, Bridge will be authorised to provide digital asset custody, stablecoin issuance, orchestration, and reserve management services.

While the timeline for full approval remains undisclosed, this charter will allow Bridge to operate its suite of stablecoin products and services under direct federal oversight.

"Fully reserved, transparently managed digital dollars can power global settlement, treasury management, cross-border payments, tokenised asset markets, and many more use cases for users globally," states Bridge.

## Mashreq Neo global head Radu Topliceanu departs

A leadership transition is underway at Mashreq's digital bank Neo as global head Radu Topliceanu announces his departure.

In a statement posted to LinkedIn, the outgoing executive writes: "After 3+ years as global head of Neo at Mashreq, I'm moving on — back to Europe for my next chapter, to lead retail banking transformation."

Neo was launched by UAE-headquartered Mashreq in 2017, offering loans, cards, and money transfers, and has been led by Topliceanu since the beginning of 2023.

Under his leadership, Neo's balance sheet increased threefold, boosted by a services expansion to Egypt, as well as a successful go-live executed in Pakistan late last year.

As for next career steps, Topliceanu concludes his departure statement saying: "If you're building the next wave of retail banking in Europe/MENA, digital with human touch, I'm open to exchanging notes."
